# Blogs & News (2026-05-12)

## Top Stories

**[Empowerment, corrigibility, etc. are simple abstractions (of a messed-up ontology)](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/vzHtHHBJoKATi5SeK/empowerment-corrigibility-etc-are-simple-abstractions-of-a)** - A new Alignment Forum post challenges fundamental concepts in AI alignment, arguing that desiderata like empowerment, corrigibility, and avoiding manipulation face a core problem: human goals themselves are underdetermined and manipulable, making it difficult to distinguish between beneficial goal changes ("providing counsel") and harmful manipulation. This strikes at the heart of current alignment frameworks that assume we can meaningfully define "helping humans achieve their goals."

**[Clarifying the role of the behavioral selection model](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/nBjo6yniFjmy2uaYa/clarifying-the-role-of-the-behavioral-selection-model)** - An elaboration on the behavioral selection model for predicting AI motivations emphasizes that similar training behaviors can lead to radically different deployment outcomes depending on underlying motivations. This highlights a critical challenge for AI safety: identical surface-level performance during training may mask fundamentally different internal drives that only become apparent in deployment scenarios.

**[Measuring the Self-Reported Impact of Early-2026 AI on Technical Worker Productivity](https://metr.org/blog/2026-05-11-ai-usage-survey/)** - METR released new survey data on how AI systems are affecting technical worker productivity in early 2026, providing empirical evidence for ongoing discussions about AI's economic impact and capabilities growth. This data is crucial for calibrating expectations about current AI capabilities and informing discussions about economic displacement and productivity gains.

**[How useful is the information you get from working inside an AI company?](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/84TtjdeLcDTtCLYaP/how-useful-is-the-information-you-get-from-working-inside-an-2)** - A quantitative analysis comparing the value of insider access to AI companies versus simply waiting for public information, operationalized by comparing employee access to a "crystal ball" showing future public knowledge. This analysis is important for understanding information asymmetries in AI safety and whether safety researchers should prioritize industry positions for access to non-public information.

**[Import AI 456: RSI and economic growth; radical optionality for AI regulation; and a neural computer](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-456-rsi-and-economic-growth)** - Jack Clark's latest newsletter covers recursive self-improvement (RSI) and its relationship to economic growth, alongside discussions of regulatory approaches and neural computing developments. This synthesis provides important context for understanding how superintelligence scenarios might unfold and what regulatory frameworks might be needed.

## Policy & Governance

**[Commission details rules to label deepfakes and chatbots](https://www.heise.de/news/KI-Transparenz-EU-Kommission-konkretisiert-Regeln-gegen-digitale-Taeuschung-11290417.html?wt_mc=rss.red.ho.ho.atom.beitrag.beitrag)** - The EU Commission published draft guidelines for Article 50 of the EU AI Act, defining transparency requirements for four categories: interactive systems, AI-generated content, emotion/biometric tools, and deepfakes on public-interest topics, with machine-readable labels and clear disclosures required by August 2026. This represents concrete implementation of AI transparency requirements that could serve as a model for other jurisdictions and establishes precedent for mandatory AI disclosure standards.
