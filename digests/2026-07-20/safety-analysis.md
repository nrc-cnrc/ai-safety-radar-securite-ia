# Blogs & News (2026-07-20)

## Top Stories

**[Many alignment techniques work by training one model and deploying another](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/syAbdNei8BWeP2RPo/many-alignment-techniques-work-by-training-one-model-and)** introduces the concept of "train-deploy mismatch" as a unifying framework for understanding steering vectors, inoculation prompting, and post-hoc honesty fine-tuning. The author argues these methods all face the same fundamental tradeoff between training data relevance and method efficacy. This matters because it reveals a potentially problematic pattern in current alignment research where we may be building techniques that rely on configuration differences that could fail during deployment.

**[Endogenous Alignment](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/xWZpwPPp5nR9rZq3x/endogenous-alignment)** draws parallels between child development and AI alignment, arguing that humans develop alignment through both external conditioning and internal social instincts that make them receptive to value learning. The post suggests that effective AI alignment may require building systems with similar intrinsic motivations rather than relying solely on external training methods. This matters because it challenges the predominant focus on reward-based training and suggests we may need fundamentally different approaches that incorporate intrinsic social drives.

**[A Red Line and Oversight Framework for Government AI Contracts](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/CCt9oy8axcdvaGcPE/a-red-line-and-oversight-framework-for-government-ai)** proposes specific guidelines for government AI procurement, including prohibitions on autonomous targeting without human control and untargeted profiling, while allowing defensive applications like missile defense. The framework attempts to balance security needs with safety constraints through careful mechanism design. This matters because it provides concrete policy tools for implementing responsible AI governance in high-stakes government applications where the consequences of misalignment could be severe.

**[Demis Hassabis on the New Coming Age](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/demis-hassabis-on-the-new-coming)** analyzes Google CEO Demis Hassabis's recent essay "A Framework for Frontier AI and the Dawning of a New Age," offering critical commentary on his vision for AI development and governance. This matters because Hassabis leads one of the world's most influential AI labs, and his public statements on AI safety and development timelines significantly shape both industry practices and policy discussions.
