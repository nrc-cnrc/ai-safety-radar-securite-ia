# Blogs & News (2026-06-01)

## Top Stories

**[Notes on axes of variation in third-party risk assessment](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/oGTajcGwxxFG4Z7eN/notes-on-axes-of-variation-in-third-party-risk-assessment)** - A detailed analysis of different approaches to third-party AI risk assessment, examining key distinctions between developers, stakeholders (governments, public, boards), and assessment methodologies. This framework is critical for understanding how independent AI safety evaluations might be structured as regulatory requirements emerge.

**[Financial Costs of an AI Pause?](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/YJWe9tT7LDYefK4vE/financial-costs-of-an-ai-pause)** - Economic analysis estimating that an AI development pause would cause the S&P 500 to drop 27.8% and AI subsectors to fall 34-69%, while slowing interest rate increases. This quantifies the potential economic resistance policymakers would face when considering AI development moratoriums for safety reasons.
