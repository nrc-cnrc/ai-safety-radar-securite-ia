**[Do LLMs Have Desires?](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/8GvYyqDuQDJnEAky3/do-llms-have-desires)** - Research examining whether LLMs demonstrate genuine preferences by testing if they modulate output quality based on context, finding that models do adjust effort in response to role-playing and exhortations. This matters because understanding whether LLMs have genuine preferences versus learned behaviors is crucial for alignment research and predicting advanced AI behavior.

**[Agents as Webs of Beliefs](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/M39Z2CvyfaxZdaxR4/agents-as-webs-of-beliefs)** - A theoretical framework unifying beliefs, goals, and actions as interconnected components of intelligent agents, drawing from active inference and agent foundations research. This matters because developing better theoretical models of agency could improve our ability to understand and align advanced AI systems.

**[Latest open artifacts (#22): Zyphra, Cohere, and Poolside are expanding the breadth of the ecosystem](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/artifacts-22-zyphra-cohere-and-poolside)** - Nathan Lambert assesses recent open model releases and the strategic motivations behind companies choosing to release models openly versus keeping them proprietary. This matters because the open vs. closed model dynamic significantly impacts AI governance approaches and the distribution of advanced capabilities.
