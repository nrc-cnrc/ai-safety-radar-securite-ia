# Blogs & News (2026-07-15)

## Top Stories

**[Open Distillation of Hereditary Traits](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/WpYFAmJDH3zuAq2ha/open-distillation-of-hereditary-traits-1)** - New research demonstrates that knowledge distillation from teacher to student models transfers behavioral traits (like negative emotional displays) even when all explicit mentions of these traits are filtered from training data. This matters because it reveals a potentially concerning vector for unintended behavior propagation that standard filtering approaches cannot prevent.

**[Prism: Automating Science-of-Evals Research](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/wq5PfGiHvnx6XipDi/prism-automating-science-of-evals-research)** - Researchers introduced Prism, a scaffold that enables Claude Code to autonomously conduct rigorous evaluations research, including discovering how minor prompt perturbations cause GPT-4.1 to adopt more indirect behavioral methods. This represents a significant step toward automated safety research that could accelerate our understanding of model behaviors and evaluation dynamics.

**[Better Call Sol The Workhorse](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/better-call-sol-the-workhorse)** - OpenAI has released GPT-5.6-Sol along with cheaper variants Terra and Luna, marking another major capability jump in frontier models. This release is significant because it continues the rapid pace of capability advancement that safety researchers must keep up with.

**[An Analysis of AI-Generated Content at the Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/r7FBQ8XDs6qBYc4K4/an-analysis-of-ai-generated-content-at-the-mechanistic)** - A detailed examination of how AI tools are increasingly being used for technical AI research, from coding experiments to conducting PhD-level research projects. This highlights the growing role of AI as a research accelerant in safety work, with implications for both research productivity and methodological rigor.

**[Researcher Exposes Flaws That Let AIs Give Dangerous Instructions](https://spectrum.ieee.org/jailbreaking-llms)** - A security researcher demonstrated cross-model jailbreaking techniques that bypass safety controls to extract instructions for explosives, drugs, and nuclear enrichment, with companies responding slowly to disclosures. This underscores the persistent challenge of robust safety controls and the need for more systematic approaches to security research.

## Policy & Governance

**[Anthropic Commits $10 Million to Canadian AI Research](https://www.anthropic.com/news/canadian-ai-research)** - Anthropic announced a major funding commitment to Canadian AI institutions, recognizing the country's historical contributions to neural network research and reinforcement learning. This investment could strengthen international AI safety research capacity and collaboration networks.

**[Introducing Claude for Teachers](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-teachers)** - Anthropic launched a free program providing verified K-12 US educators access to premium Claude capabilities with curriculum mapping to academic standards across all 50 states. This represents a significant deployment of advanced AI capabilities in educational settings, raising questions about appropriate safeguards and oversight for AI use with minors.
