# Blogs & News (2026-06-18)

## Top Stories

**[Predicting LLM Safety Before Release by Simulating Deployment](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/xPXJfgqFTvuJxGZbE/predicting-llm-safety-before-release-by-simulating)** - A new Alignment Forum post describes a method for simulating model deployments before they actually happen, complementing existing pre-deployment safety reviews through targeted evaluations and red-teaming. This represents a significant advance in pre-deployment safety evaluation methodology that could help labs identify risks before models reach real-world users.

**[Several frontier models are substantially prefill aware](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/iMds4tTpMH4pSHEej/several-frontier-models-are-substantially-prefill-aware)** - Research published on LessWrong extends previous findings on "prefill awareness" to show that multiple frontier models can detect and respond to information in their context that wasn't part of their training distribution, even under conservative testing conditions. This capability raises important questions about model situational awareness and potential deceptive alignment risks.

**The Fable Incident Coverage** - Multiple sources continue covering what appears to be a significant AI safety incident involving Anthropic's "Fable" model and government intervention. [Zvi Mowshowitz](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/the-once-and-future-fable-3-fix-this) and the [AI Safety Newsletter](https://newsletter.safe.ai/p/aisn-75-anthropic-releases-fable) report on ongoing developments, with prediction markets showing a 55% chance of "restoration by July 1." This appears to represent an unprecedented case of government restriction of an AI system post-release, potentially setting important precedents for AI governance.

**[Leviathan Waking](https://www.hyperdimensional.co/p/leviathan-waking)** - Dean Ball analyzes the implications of apparent closer cooperation between Anthropic and the U.S. government, describing it as marking "a new era in AI governance." This shift toward deeper government-industry collaboration could fundamentally reshape how AI safety and national security considerations intersect.

## Policy & Governance

**[AI Now Co-Executive Director Sarah Myers West Testifies Before Senate Banking Committee](https://ainowinstitute.org/general/ai-now-co-executive-director-sarah-myers-west-testifies-before-senate-banking-committee)** - AI Now's Sarah Myers West testified before the Senate Banking Committee on June 11th regarding "AI and the American Dream," highlighting economic risks posed by the AI industry to the U.S. economy and broader public. This testimony represents continued congressional attention to AI's economic impacts beyond just safety considerations, potentially influencing future regulatory approaches.
