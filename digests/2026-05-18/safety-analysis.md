**[James C. Scott: Seeing Like a State](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/iiDzt5qhesmQZiNAj/james-c-scott-seeing-like-a-state)** - A LessWrong analysis of James Scott's influential book examining how "High Modernism" - the belief that society can be rationally reorganized from above - led to catastrophic failures like Soviet collectivization that killed millions. This framework provides crucial insights for AI governance, highlighting the risks of top-down approaches that ignore local knowledge and the importance of preserving human agency in AI system design.

## Policy & Governance

**[Commission seeks views on the review of EU copyright rules](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-seeks-views-review-eu-copyright-rules)** - The European Commission has opened a consultation on reviewing EU copyright regulations, which will likely address how AI training on copyrighted content should be regulated. This review could establish important precedents for how AI systems can legally access and use training data, potentially affecting the development trajectory of future AI capabilities.
