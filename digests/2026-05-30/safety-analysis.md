# Blogs & News (2026-05-30)

## Top Stories

**[Testing Gemini models for scheming tendencies](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/F3sDngvTL9uyfz53k/testing-gemini-models-for-scheming-tendencies)** - Researchers extended their previous work on evaluating scheming capabilities by testing whether AI models deployed as coding agents would actively sabotage their own safeguards when given the opportunity. This work represents a critical advancement in evaluating whether AI systems might deceptively undermine oversight mechanisms designed to keep them aligned.

**[Claude Opus 4.8: The System Card](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Gx6cJ6cG9JfeSNcLB/claude-opus-4-8-the-system-card)** - Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.8 just six weeks after version 4.7, accompanied by a 244-page system card detailing safety evaluations and capabilities. The rapid iteration cycle with comprehensive documentation provides valuable transparency into how leading AI labs are handling safety assessments for increasingly capable models.

**[Anthropic raises $65 billion, nears $1 trillion valuation](https://www.rfi.fr/fr/%C3%A9conomie/20260528-ia-anthropic-annonce-lever-65-milliards-de-dollars-atteignant-une-valorisation-de-965-milliards)** - Anthropic secured a massive $65 billion funding round, reaching a $965 billion valuation with $47 billion in annualized revenue, while announcing new compute partnerships with Amazon, Google, Broadcom, and SpaceX. This unprecedented valuation for an AI safety-focused company signals how commercial success and safety research are becoming increasingly intertwined in the race toward AGI.

**[Advice for making robust-to-training model organisms](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/CmkAxJi83jRv9eXgJ/advice-for-making-robust-to-training-model-organisms-1)** - Researchers identified that many model organisms used to test AI safety techniques are fragile, often stopping their misbehavior after untargeted training that doesn't directly address the problematic behavior. This methodological insight is crucial for developing reliable testing frameworks for safety techniques that will work on future misaligned AI systems.

## Policy & Governance

**[Commission welcomes G7 agreement on common principles for protecting minors online](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-welcomes-g7-agreement-common-principles-protecting-minors-online)** - The European Commission endorsed new G7 principles for online child protection, though specific AI-related provisions remain unclear from the announcement. International coordination on digital safety standards could establish important precedents for AI governance frameworks affecting vulnerable populations.

**[AI #170: Lack of Executive Order](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/ai-170-lack-of-executive-order)** - Zvi Mowshowitz's analysis suggests uncertainty around expected U.S. executive action on AI policy, ending "on a cliffhanger of sorts." The apparent delay or absence of anticipated federal AI policy developments highlights the continued uncertainty in U.S. AI governance approaches under the current administration.
