# Research Papers (2026-06-03)

## Key Papers

### AI Agents and Tool Use

[**AI Agents Enable Adaptive Computer Worms**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03811v1) demonstrates a concerning new threat where AI agents create self-adapting malware that generates tailored attack strategies for each target using compromised machines to run language models. Unlike traditional worms with fixed vulnerabilities, these AI-powered worms continuously adapt their strategies, making them significantly harder to defend against. This research highlights critical security implications as AI agents become more autonomous and capable.

[**Overlaying Governance: A Compositional Authorization Framework for Delegation and Scope in Agentic AI**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03518v1) addresses the governance challenge of autonomous AI agents by proposing a compositional authorization framework that handles permission inheritance, delegation, and time-limited authority across agent hierarchies. The work tackles a fundamental safety problem: how to maintain control and accountability as AI systems become more autonomous and collaborative. This framework is essential for safe deployment of agentic systems in production environments.

[**Agentic Chain-of-Thought Steering for Efficient and Controllable LLM Reasoning**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03965v1) introduces ACTS, a Markov decision process approach where a controller agent adaptively steers the reasoning process of large language models during chain-of-thought generation. This method offers inference-time control over how models think, potentially improving both efficiency and reliability of AI reasoning.

### AI Safety and Robustness

[**VLESA: Vision-Language Embodied Safety Agent for Human Activity Monitoring**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03954v1) presents a framework that monitors human activities from egocentric video and triggers real-time safety interventions when dangerous actions are predicted, addressing intent-dependent safety where identical actions can be safe or dangerous depending on context. This work represents an important step toward AI systems that can actively prevent harm in physical environments, particularly relevant as AI becomes more integrated into safety-critical applications.

[**Backdoor Unlearning Generalization: A Path Toward the Removal of Unknown Triggers in LLMs**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03785v1) shows that training models to ignore a single backdoor trigger can also suppress other unknown backdoors, offering hope for defending against hidden vulnerabilities in deployed language models. This finding suggests that backdoor defenses may generalize better than previously thought, providing a path toward more robust AI systems.

[**Exploring Adversarial Robustness and Safety Alignment in Multilingual Multi-Modal Large Language Models**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03793v1) conducts the first systematic study of adversarial robustness across 12 languages in multimodal language models, revealing significant vulnerabilities in multilingual settings that could undermine safety alignment efforts. This research exposes critical gaps in current safety measures as AI systems become more globally deployed.

### Knowledge and Reasoning

[**Quantifying Faithful Confidence Expression in Large Reasoning Models**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03969v1) addresses the critical problem of faithful calibration in large reasoning models—the alignment between models' intrinsic confidence and their linguistically expressed confidence—finding that extended reasoning traces are often misinterpreted as evidence of competence. This work is crucial for building trustworthy AI systems where users can accurately assess model uncertainty.

[**Don't Forget Your Embeddings: Robust Knowledge Erasure via Precise Editing of Embeddings**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03695v1) introduces EMBedding ERasure (EMBER), demonstrating that existing knowledge editing methods fail because they overlook the embedding layer, and shows that targeting embeddings directly achieves more persistent knowledge removal. This research is vital for AI safety applications requiring reliable removal of sensitive or harmful information from deployed models.
