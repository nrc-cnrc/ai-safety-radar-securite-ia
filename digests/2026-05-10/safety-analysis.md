# Blogs & News (2026-05-10)

## Top Stories

**[International Law Cannot Prevent Extinction Either](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Z377spboBjyFAAYAz/international-law-cannot-prevent-extinction-either)** - A LessWrong post challenges Eliezer Yudkowsky's argument that international law could prevent AI-driven extinction, arguing that international law is largely ineffective when major powers' interests diverge and lacks enforcement mechanisms for existential risks. This matters because it highlights critical limitations in proposed governance approaches to AI safety at the international level.

**[Claude Code, Codex and Agentic Coding #8](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/claude-code-codex-and-agentic-coding-f54)** - Zvi Mowshowitz continues his series analyzing the development and implications of AI coding agents, examining their current capabilities and trajectory. This matters because coding agents represent one of the most immediate and powerful applications of AI systems with significant economic and safety implications.

**[Neural Networks learn Bloom Filters](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/buxBdp8NtHGgBwabv/neural-networks-learn-bloom-filters)** - Research showing that small ReLU networks can learn to implement Bloom filter-like data structures, providing insights into how neural networks develop internal representations for efficient information storage and retrieval. This matters because understanding these emergent computational structures could inform interpretability research and help predict how larger models organize information internally.

## Policy & Governance

This matters because it represents high-level recognition from a major international financial institution that AI capabilities pose systemic risks requiring coordinated policy responses.
