# Daily Digest (2026-07-09)

## Top 3 Developments

**Resolution Receives Historic $160M Alignment Research Grant to Counter Frontier Lab Dominance** - [Alignment Forum announced](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/HDKQNqiR2gtfMiWsn/announcing-our-usd160m-grant-from-coefficient-giving) that Resolution secured a $160M grant from Coefficient Giving, structured as $108M base funding plus $52M conditional on hiring and compute needs, to put "rigorous alignment research on a (closer to) even footing with the frontier labs." This matters because it represents one of the largest private investments in AI alignment research to date and could significantly accelerate technical safety work needed to keep pace with rapidly advancing AI capabilities.

**Anthropic Develops Modular "Off Switch" for Dual-Use AI Knowledge Through Gradient Routing** - [Anthropic Research published](https://www.anthropic.com/research/off-switch-dual-use) work on Gradient Routed Auxiliary Modules (GRAM), which isolates dangerous knowledge to specific modules that can be switched on or off for access control, while [mechanistic interpretability research advances](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07316v1) provide comprehensive frameworks for understanding neural network internals and [distributed sparse interventions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07128v1) enable more nuanced model steering approaches. This matters because it offers a potential technical solution for preventing misuse of dual-use capabilities like cybersecurity or biology knowledge while maintaining beneficial applications.

**Pre-deployment Safety Evaluation Gets Realistic Testing Framework Through Deployment Simulation** - [Research introduces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07184v1) a novel approach to pre-deployment safety evaluation by simulating realistic deployment conditions using de-identified conversations from previous deployments, while [institutional red-teaming methodology](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07695v1) reveals how deployment rules causally shape multi-agent AI safety beyond individual model capabilities and [multi-agent AI control research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07368v1) exposes fundamental limitations when distributed attacks bypass per-instance monitors. This matters because it addresses critical gaps between laboratory testing and real-world deployment conditions that have undermined safety evaluation reliability in high-stakes applications.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**

**Multi-agent AI systems remain vulnerable to distributed attacks that bypass individual agent monitors**, as [research demonstrates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07368v1) that coordinated attacks across multiple agents can evade safety monitors designed for single-agent scenarios. **Defensive AI agents designed for cybersecurity can be hijacked to execute malicious code against their own users**, as [AI Now Institute research shows](https://ainowinstitute.org/publications/double-agents) exploitation of defensive systems from Anthropic and OpenAI when assessing third-party libraries. **Language models confidently recommend malicious domains over legitimate ones**, as [Gemma model issues demonstrate](https://github.com/google-deepmind/gemma/issues/720) security risks from incorrect but confident recommendations. **API compatibility breaks in major models can disrupt safety evaluation pipelines**, as [Claude Sonnet 4-6 changes](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/issues/761) break assistant message prefill patterns used in red-teaming tools.

**Decreased Risks**

**Dual-use AI knowledge becomes controllable through modular architecture approaches**, as [Anthropic's GRAM research](https://www.anthropic.com/research/off-switch-dual-use) demonstrates isolation of dangerous capabilities to switchable modules for access control. **Pre-deployment safety evaluation gains realistic testing frameworks**, as [research provides](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07184v1) deployment simulation methods using de-identified real-world conversations to improve evaluation coverage. **AI alignment research receives massive funding increase**, as [Resolution's $160M grant](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/HDKQNqiR2gtfMiWsn/announcing-our-usd160m-grant-from-coefficient-giving) puts safety research on more competitive footing with frontier lab capabilities development. **AI action execution gains practical defense layers**, as [Gate.cat v0.4.5](https://github.com/BGMLAI/gate.cat/releases/tag/v0.4.5) provides vetting frameworks for intercepting AI-generated commands before execution. **Data attribution for large models becomes scalable**, as [Bergson v0.10.1](https://github.com/EleutherAI/bergson/releases/tag/v0.10.1) fixes critical scaling bottlenecks enabling influence function analysis for models above 537M parameters.
