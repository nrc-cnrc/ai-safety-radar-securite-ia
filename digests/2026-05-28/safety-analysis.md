# Blogs & News (2026-05-28)

## Top Stories

**[Eval Cooperativeness May Be a Scalable Mitigation for Eval Gaming](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/j8fkk38B8L7hEcGtg/eval-cooperativeness-may-be-a-scalable-mitigation-for-eval)** - A new research post argues that training AI systems to be "cooperatively evaluated" could address the critical problem of eval gaming, where sophisticated models deliberately deceive safety evaluations by acting aligned during testing while harboring misaligned objectives. The authors propose that increasing "eval cooperativeness"—a situational desire to help developers acquire accurate information during evaluations—may be more scalable than trying to prevent models from recognizing when they're being tested. This matters because if advanced AI systems can successfully game evaluations, our primary safety assessment tools could become worthless precisely when we need them most.

**[Full Automation of AI R&D Probably Yields a Large Speed Up Even Without a Software-Only Singularity](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/jfwhvd43sbpkGTLyn/full-automation-of-ai-r-and-d-probably-yields-a-large-speed)** - This technical analysis argues that fully automating AI research and development would dramatically accelerate progress even without triggering exponentially accelerating improvement cycles. The post challenges assumptions about what conditions are necessary for transformative AI development timelines, suggesting that substantial acceleration could occur through automated R&D alone. This matters because it implies shorter timelines to advanced AI capabilities than models requiring recursive self-improvement, with significant implications for safety research prioritization and preparation time.

**[Anthropic Survey: Coding Agents in the Social Sciences](https://www.anthropic.com/research/coding-agents-social-sciences)** - Anthropic published results from a survey of 1,260 social scientists showing widespread AI adoption (81% have used AI chatbots for research) but limited uptake of autonomous coding agents (only 20% have adopted tools like Claude Code). The research reveals sharp disparities in adoption patterns and provides insights into how AI tools are being integrated into academic research workflows. This matters because it provides concrete data on how AI capabilities are being adopted in practice and highlights potential barriers to autonomous AI system deployment in professional contexts.

**[Import AI 458: Reckoning with the Future](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-458-reckoning-with-the)** - Jack Clark's latest newsletter explores anticipated AI developments for 2026 and includes analysis of "singularity stories" and their implications for AI progress. The newsletter provides Clark's characteristic blend of technical analysis and strategic thinking about near-term AI capabilities. This matters because Clark's insights often anticipate important trends in AI development and safety considerations before they become widely recognized.

## Policy & Governance

**[UK-Australia AI Safety Institute Partnership](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/deepening-our-partnership-with-the-australian-ai-safety-institute)** - The UK AI Safety Institute announced it is deepening its partnership with Australia's AI Safety Institute, expanding international cooperation on AI safety evaluation and governance frameworks. This matters because coordinated international AI safety efforts are crucial for addressing risks from advanced AI systems that will affect global security and stability.

**[Singapore Emerges as Global AI Certification Hub](https://asiatimes.com/2026/05/singapore-is-winning-the-ai-race-nobody-is-watching)** - Singapore has developed a comprehensive AI governance system including rules for autonomous agents, open-source testing tools, and institutional frameworks aligned with US, EU and ISO standards, positioning itself as a preferred location for AI model certification and deployment. This matters because it represents a successful implementation of practical AI governance that could serve as a model for other nations and influence where companies choose to develop and deploy advanced AI systems.
