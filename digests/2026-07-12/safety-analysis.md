# Blogs & News (2026-07-12)

## Top Stories

**Political Will, Not Research, Is Now AI Safety's Main Bottleneck**

A provocative post argues that [the current bottleneck is political will, not research](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/EexsebbYhbe2gXkPP/the-current-bottleneck-is-political-will-not-research), claiming we already possess sufficient knowledge and policy frameworks to significantly reduce AI risks. The author contends that best practices exist but aren't being applied or enforced because policymakers lack awareness of the problem's severity. This challenges the conventional wisdom that more research is needed before effective action can be taken.

**AI Futures Project Releases "Plan A" Strategic Framework**

The AI Futures Project has released ["AI 2040: Plan A"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/B4qMZnB4a5YgKiLNo/plan-a-suggestions-for-further-work) alongside a call for further research into key strategic questions about navigating the intelligence explosion. Expert commentator Zvi Mowshowitz has provided both an [introduction and reaction to Plan A](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/introduction-for-and-reactions-to) as well as coverage of [Plan B alternatives](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/ai-176-part-2-plan-b). This represents a significant attempt to chart concrete pathways through transformative AI development scenarios.

**Value Correction: A New Approach to AI Alignment** 

Researchers are exploring [value correction](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/iPyJfD9Jyxj6Jfdws/value-generalisation-value-correction) as a key mechanism for AI alignment, focusing on agents that can recognize when their reward functions are incorrect and act to fix them. The approach examines how systems trained in-distribution can generalize their values when encountering out-of-distribution scenarios. This technical work addresses one of the core challenges in ensuring AI systems remain aligned as they encounter novel situations.

**Call for Total Research Transparency in AI Development**

Ajeya Cotra and Kelsey Piper argue that [total research transparency would be beneficial](https://www.planned-obsolescence.org/p/total-research-transparency-would) for identifying dangerous research directions before they cause harm. Their proposal suggests that complete openness about AI research could help the field collectively identify and avoid hazardous paths. This contributes to ongoing debates about information sharing versus security considerations in AI development.

## Policy & Governance

**Georgetown CSET Director Addresses US-China AI Competition**

CSET Executive Director Helen Toner [discussed the global AI race](https://cset.georgetown.edu/article/helen-toner-discusses-us-china-ai-race-at-aspen-ideas-festival/) at the Aspen Ideas Festival, providing expert analysis on competitive dynamics between major AI powers. This high-profile forum discussion highlights how AI competition considerations are increasingly central to policy conversations at senior levels.
