# Research Papers (2026-07-12)

## Key Papers

**[Revising research practices for singing data collection](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-026-03205-4)** - AI & Society (Springer)

This peer-reviewed paper examines the urgent governance challenges posed by AI voice synthesis capabilities, particularly focusing on singing datasets that enable sophisticated vocal deepfakes and non-consensual voice cloning. The authors analyze how current data collection practices inadequately protect data contributors from downstream harms, including unauthorized use of their vocal signatures for synthetic content generation. This work is critical as it addresses a concrete near-term AI safety challenge where existing systems already pose real risks to individuals, highlighting the need for proactive data governance frameworks that can keep pace with rapidly advancing synthesis capabilities.
