**[A (Slightly) Mechanistic Theory for Exponentially Increasing AI Time Horizons?](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/zT76JcomKkdqo8tC6/a-slightly-mechanistic-theory-for-exponentially-increasing)** - A LessWrong analysis examines METR's influential 2025 graph showing AI systems achieving longer "time horizons" in planning and execution tasks, arguing that these improvements are primarily driven by increased training data rather than temporal reasoning capabilities. **[Trump delays federal review of advanced models](https://www.heise.de/news/Trump-stoppt-wichtige-Verordnung-fuer-KI-Modelle-11305450.html?wt_mc=rss.red.ho.ho.atom.beitrag.beitrag)** - President Trump postponed signing regulations that would have given U.S. agencies up to 90 days to review advanced AI models before release, citing competitiveness concerns after pressure from tech leaders and investor David Sacks. This represents a significant rollback of AI safety oversight that could accelerate the deployment of powerful models like Anthropic's Claude Mythos without adequate security review.

## Policy & Governance

The Trump administration's decision to delay federal AI model review procedures marks a major shift away from the previous administration's approach to AI governance. The postponed rule was specifically designed to address cybersecurity risks from advanced AI systems and would have provided government agencies with advance notice of powerful model releases. This policy reversal suggests a prioritization of industry competitiveness over safety evaluation processes, potentially creating gaps in oversight of increasingly capable AI systems.
