# Blogs & News (2026-07-01)

## Top Stories

**Anthropic Launches Claude Science Workbench and Redeploys Fable 5 After Export Controls**

Anthropic has made several major announcements, including the launch of [Claude Science, an AI workbench for scientists](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-science-ai-workbench) and the [redeployment of Claude Fable 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5) after a two-week suspension due to US export controls. The company also released [Claude Sonnet 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-sonnet-5), described as their "most agentic Sonnet model yet" with capabilities previously requiring larger, more expensive models. These developments signal both the growing specialized applications of frontier AI and the increasing intersection of AI development with national security policy.

**Chinese API Relay Networks Circumvent Western AI Access Controls**

A [detailed analysis on LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/YrgeED3nWD4EjcqLd/the-consequences-of-locking-intelligence-away-an) reveals how Chinese entrepreneurs are using Western VC-subsidized AI credits and subscriptions to create API relay stations that resell access to models like Claude and ChatGPT to domestic Chinese users, bypassing official restrictions. This trend has accelerated significantly since 2024-2025, with relay services readily available on platforms like Taobao. This highlights the practical challenges of implementing AI export controls and the potential for unintended market dynamics when advanced AI capabilities are restricted geographically.

**MIRI Launches AI StopWatch News Channel**

The Machine Intelligence Research Institute announced the launch of [AI StopWatch](https://intelligence.org/2026/06/30/miri-newsletter-126/), a dedicated daily news and analysis channel focused on AI developments. MIRI also published a [summary of their Technical Governance Team's papers](https://intelligence.org/2026/06/30/summary-tgts-2026-icml-papers/) for the upcoming ICML conference and its Technical AI Governance Research workshop. This represents MIRI's expanded focus on real-time AI governance analysis alongside their traditional AI alignment research.

**Zvi Challenges WSJ Claims About Chinese AI Capabilities**

AI commentator Zvi Mowshowitz published a [sharp critique](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/wsj-article-claiming-china-has-matched) of a Wall Street Journal article claiming China has matched Anthropic's capabilities, calling it "obvious nonsense" with a "false headline and heavily misleading story." This reflects ongoing debates about accurately assessing international AI capabilities and the risk of both over- and under-estimating competitors' progress.

## Policy & Governance

**US Export Controls on Frontier AI Models Show Mixed Implementation**

The recent export control episode involving Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 - where [controls were applied on June 12 and lifted by June 30](https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5) - demonstrates both the US government's willingness to use export controls as a policy tool for frontier AI and the challenges of real-time implementation. The fact that Anthropic had to suspend access entirely due to inability to verify user nationality in real-time reveals significant operational gaps in the current export control framework for AI services.
