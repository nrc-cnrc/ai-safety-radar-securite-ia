# Blogs & News (2026-06-15)

## Top Stories

**Google DeepMind reveals SFT's central role in AI safety** - The Google DeepMind Language Model Interpretability team published two significant findings: [SFT Drives Gemini's Safety Properties](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/nLrrYweeFxgXACSmS/sft-drives-gemini-s-safety-properties-1) shows that most safety-relevant properties in Gemini models come from supervised fine-tuning (SFT) combined with pretraining, rather than reinforcement learning stages, while [Why Do Naive SFT Filters For Safety Properties Fail?](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/wyZRNgpeiPeRXB6eT/why-do-naive-sft-filters-for-safety-properties-fail) demonstrates that filtering SFT training data for undesirable properties works surprisingly poorly. These findings challenge common assumptions about where safety properties originate in modern AI systems and suggest current data filtering approaches may be fundamentally inadequate.

**Government action against Claude sparks governance concerns** - Zvi Mowshowitz reports on [American Government Takes Down Claude Fable](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/american-government-takes-down-claude), describing what appears to be government action against Anthropic's Claude system announced late Friday evening. This represents a potential escalation in direct government intervention in AI development and deployment decisions.

**AI governance enters uncharted territory** - Nathan Lambert's [Welcome to the AGI era of AI governance](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/welcome-to-the-agi-era-of-ai-governance) argues that we've crossed a "one-way door" into AGI-level governance challenges without adequate preparation. This analysis highlights the growing gap between AI capabilities advancement and governance readiness at a critical inflection point.

## Policy & Governance

**EU expands AI governance infrastructure** - The European Commission announced it is [seeking leading academics for the RAISE High-Level Academic Advisory Board](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-seeks-leading-academics-raise-high-level-academic-advisory-board), expanding its academic advisory capacity for AI policy development. This move signals the EU's continued effort to build robust institutional capacity for AI governance as systems become more powerful and consequential.
