# Blogs & News (2026-06-14)

## Top Stories

**Google DeepMind reveals SFT drives most safety properties in Gemini models**  
The Google DeepMind Language Model Interpretability team [published findings](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/nLrrYweeFxgXACSmS/sft-drives-gemini-s-safety-properties-1) showing that most safety-relevant properties in Gemini models stem from the combination of pretraining and supervised fine-tuning (SFT), rather than reinforcement learning or other training stages. This challenges conventional wisdom about where safety properties originate in modern language models and could significantly impact how AI labs approach safety training.

**New "diffing agents" can reliably find behavioral differences between AI models**  
Google DeepMind researchers [demonstrated](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/qi4mNbZYAFDYwfRba/building-and-evaluating-model-diffing-agents) that simple AI agents can systematically identify interesting behavioral differences between distinct models, going beyond static dataset comparisons to dynamic behavioral analysis. This technique could become crucial for safety evaluations as it enables more comprehensive understanding of how model changes affect behavior.

**US government classifies Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 under export controls**  
The Department of Commerce [reportedly classified](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/american-government-takes-down-claude) Anthropic's new Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models under export controls, cutting off access to foreign nationals even within the US, apparently in response to a narrow jailbreak identified by Amazon. This marks a significant escalation in government oversight of advanced AI systems and sets a precedent for rapid regulatory responses to capability advances.

**Continual learning poses new safety challenges for deployed AI systems**  
Researchers [analyzed](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/j2zBqt7AksoEoHXNp/how-might-continual-learning-affect-safety-and-alignment) how continual learning could undermine current safety approaches by enabling goal and value changes after deployment and eliminating the "last-mover advantage" that current safety interventions rely on. This highlights a critical gap in safety frameworks as AI systems become more adaptive and autonomous post-deployment.

**Debate intensifies over "egregious misalignment" scenarios**  
A new analysis [explores](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/DZaZ3fqHnvfLCftPu/sympathy-for-both-sides-of-the-egregious-misalignment-debate) the growing divide between researchers who expect catastrophic misalignment from superintelligence (like Yudkowsky and Soares) and most LLM researchers who are more optimistic about alignment prospects. This fundamental disagreement about AI risk trajectories continues to shape research priorities and policy discussions across the field.
