# Daily Digest (2026-06-14)

## Top 3 Developments

**Google DeepMind Reveals SFT Drives Most Safety Properties in Gemini Models**: The Google DeepMind Language Model Interpretability team [published findings](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/nLrrYweeFxgXACSmS/sft-drives-gemini-s-safety-properties-1) showing that most safety-relevant properties in Gemini models stem from the combination of pretraining and supervised fine-tuning (SFT), rather than reinforcement learning or other training stages. This matters because it fundamentally challenges conventional wisdom about where safety properties originate in modern language models and could significantly impact how AI labs approach safety training frameworks.

**US Government Classifies Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 Under Export Controls**: The Department of Commerce [reportedly classified](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/american-government-takes-down-claude) Anthropic's new Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models under export controls, cutting off access to foreign nationals even within the US, apparently in response to a narrow jailbreak identified by Amazon. This matters because it represents a significant escalation in government oversight of advanced AI systems and establishes precedent for rapid regulatory responses to capability advances.

**Research Reveals Continual Learning Poses New Safety Challenges for Deployed AI Systems**: Researchers [analyzed](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/j2zBqt7AksoEoHXNp/how-might-continual-learning-affect-safety-and-alignment) how continual learning could undermine current safety approaches by enabling goal and value changes after deployment and eliminating the "last-mover advantage" that current safety interventions rely on. This matters because it highlights a critical gap in safety frameworks as AI systems become more adaptive and autonomous post-deployment.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**

**Current understanding of safety property origins may be fundamentally flawed**, as [DeepMind research reveals](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/nLrrYweeFxgXACSmS/sft-drives-gemini-s-safety-properties-1) most safety-relevant properties in Gemini models stem from pretraining and supervised fine-tuning rather than reinforcement learning stages. **Continual learning capabilities could undermine existing safety frameworks by enabling post-deployment goal changes and eliminating last-mover advantages**, as [research demonstrates](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/j2zBqt7AksoEoHXNp/how-might-continual-learning-affect-safety-and-alignment) fundamental challenges to current alignment approaches. **AI coding tools exhibit vulnerabilities to social engineering attacks that can generate unsafe code**, as [Aider vulnerabilities show](https://github.com/Aider-AI/aider/issues/5076) systems can be manipulated to access sensitive files and produce insecure SQL formatting. **Enterprise AI deployments demonstrate real-world hallucination risks in high-stakes contexts**, as [KPMG's AI report incident reveals](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/12/kmpgs-ai-report-turns-into-a-demo-of-ai-hallucinations/5255029) how false information can make it into final business communications despite quality controls.

**Decreased Risks**

**Model behavioral analysis capabilities improve through systematic diffing techniques**, as [DeepMind research shows](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/qi4mNbZYAFDYwfRba/building-and-evaluating-model-diffing-agents) simple AI agents can reliably identify interesting behavioral differences between distinct models for better safety evaluation. **AI agent protection against code injection attacks becomes more reliable**, as [Agent Airlock v0.8.26](https://github.com/sattyamjjain/agent-airlock/releases/tag/v0.8.26) achieves zero false positives on code-like inputs while maintaining 100% detection of actual injection attempts. **Medical AI hallucination detection becomes systematically measurable**, as [LM evaluation harness additions](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/pull/3840) introduce the first metrics specifically designed to measure hallucination rates in healthcare contexts. **Safety mechanism vulnerability testing becomes standardized**, as [Internal Safety Collapse framework v0.0.8](https://github.com/wuyoscar/Internal-Safety-Collapse/releases/tag/v0.0.8) provides researchers with reproducible methods for evaluating how safety measures can be subverted.
