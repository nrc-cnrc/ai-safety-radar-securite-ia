# Blogs & News (2026-05-26)

## Top Stories

**[Many portions of Magnifica Humanitas appear to be AI-written](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/GbWwesBnetyiomxEH/many-portions-of-magnifica-humanitas-appear-to-be-ai-written)** - A LessWrong analysis suggests that Pope Leo XIV's recent encyclical on AI and human dignity contains sections that appear to be generated by AI systems themselves. The irony of using AI to write guidance on AI governance raises fundamental questions about authenticity and human agency in high-stakes communications.

**[Anthropic co-founder Chris Olah's remarks on Pope Leo XIV's encyclical "Magnifica humanitas"](https://www.anthropic.com/news/chris-olah-pope-leo-encyclical)** - Anthropic co-founder Chris Olah spoke at the Vatican presentation of the Pope's AI encyclical as part of efforts to broaden conversations about AI development beyond tech circles. This represents a significant moment in AI governance where major AI developers are directly engaging with religious institutions on questions of human dignity and AI alignment.

**[RTMH: Pope Leo's Magnifica Humanitas on AI](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/rtmh-pope-leos-magnifica-humanitas)** - AI safety commentator Zvi Mowshowitz provides analysis of the papal encyclical's positions on artificial intelligence and human relationships. The Vatican's entry into AI governance discussions signals growing recognition that AI development involves fundamental questions about human nature and social organization.

**[Trump delays federal review of advanced models](https://www.heise.de/news/Trump-stoppt-wichtige-Verordnung-fuer-KI-Modelle-11305450.html?wt_mc=rss.red.ho.ho.atom.beitrag.beitrag)** - President Trump postponed a rule that would have allowed U.S. agencies to review advanced AI models up to 90 days before release, citing competitiveness concerns after pressure from tech leaders and investor David Sacks. This delay removes a key oversight mechanism for powerful models like Anthropic's Claude Mythos at a critical moment in AI development.

**[Brackets Are a Bad Way to Regulate](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/FDxvpznjMzGNHjDRS/brackets-are-a-bad-way-to-regulate)** - A LessWrong post argues that threshold-based regulatory approaches create perverse incentives and fail to capture the continuous nature of AI capabilities and risks. This analysis is particularly relevant as policymakers worldwide struggle with how to create capability-based AI governance frameworks that don't create gaming opportunities.

## Policy & Governance

**[NIS2 Cooperation Group adopts common templates for incident reporting](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/nis2-cooperation-group-adopts-common-templates-incident-reporting)** - The EU has standardized incident reporting templates under the NIS2 directive, which could affect how AI-related cybersecurity incidents are documented and shared across member states. This creates a foundation for systematic tracking of AI safety incidents at the European level.

**[Commission publishes 2025 report on the Digital Markets Act implementation](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-publishes-2025-report-digital-markets-act-implementation)** - The European Commission released its assessment of how the Digital Markets Act has been implemented, which includes provisions affecting large AI systems and their market gatekeeping functions. This report will inform how competition policy intersects with AI governance as systems become more capable and market-dominant.
