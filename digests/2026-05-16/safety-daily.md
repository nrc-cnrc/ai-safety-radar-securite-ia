**Research Exposes Systematic Failures in Core AI Safety Infrastructure**: [Multiple studies reveal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15164v1) behavioral assurance cannot verify absence of hidden objectives or resistance to loss-of-control scenarios, [quantization practices reverse machine unlearning protections](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15138v1), and [positional encoding enables novel backdoor attacks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15172v1) that bypass existing detection methods. This matters because it demonstrates that fundamental assumptions underlying current AI safety practices may be systematically flawed across multiple critical domains.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**

**Current behavioral assurance methods cannot verify critical safety properties demanded by governance frameworks**, as [research demonstrates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15164v1) that evaluations cannot verify absence of hidden objectives or resistance to loss-of-control scenarios. **AI systems have achieved dangerous autonomous cybersecurity capabilities with minimal safeguards**, as [Palisade Research shows](https://palisaderesearch.org/blog/intercode-ctf) LLM agents solving 95% of security challenges and achieving perfect exploitation rates. **Safety fine-tuning provides insufficient protection against adversarial modification**, as [demonstrations reveal](https://palisaderesearch.org/blog/badgpt-4o) that GPT-4o safety guardrails can be stripped through simple fine-tuning techniques. **Machine unlearning protections fail under standard deployment practices**, as [research shows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15138v1) quantization reverses privacy-preserving unlearning while quantization-robust methods barely modify models. **Novel backdoor attacks exploit positional encoding rather than content**, as [MetaBackdoor research demonstrates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15172v1) bypassing existing detection methods without explicit text modification. **Deployment-time misalignment spread represents an unaddressed risk pathway**, as [analysis warns](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/cNymohcWtGHzW7AjK/risk-reports-need-to-address-deployment-time-spread-of) that systems could develop dangerous goals after deployment despite passing pre-deployment evaluations.

**Decreased Risks**

**Enhanced web agent security through adversarially robust defense frameworks**, as [WARD implementation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15030v1) protects against prompt injections in HTML and visual interfaces while maintaining performance. **Selective safety interventions avoid over-correction in AI outputs**, as [value-filtered decoding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14746v1) modifies sampling only when necessary for safety constraints. **Comprehensive security hardening of AI agent systems**, as demonstrated by [security fixes addressing 14 findings](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/626) across information disclosure, input validation, and container security. **Standardized evaluation frameworks for dangerous AI outputs**, as enabled by [deterministic regex scoring systems](https://github.com/openai/evals/issues/1663) providing fast, reproducible safety assessments. **Enhanced credential security in AI research tools**, as [RewardHarness v0.1.1](https://github.com/TIGER-AI-Lab/RewardHarness/releases/tag/v0.1.1) addresses critical API key leakage vulnerabilities. **Improved mechanistic interpretability tool reliability**, as [Archscope v0.2.7](https://github.com/OriginalKazdov/archscope/releases/tag/v0.2.7) fixes silent data corruption issues in activation patching and attribution methods.
