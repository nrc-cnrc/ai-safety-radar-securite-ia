# Research Papers (2026-05-11)

## Key Papers

### [Towards the explainability of protein language models](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-026-01232-w)
*Nature Machine Intelligence, May 11, 2026*

This peer-reviewed study advances interpretability methods for protein language models, which are increasingly used in drug discovery and biological research. The work develops novel techniques to understand how these models encode biological knowledge and make predictions about protein structure and function. This matters because as AI systems become critical tools in biotechnology and medicine, ensuring their decisions are interpretable and trustworthy is essential for both safety and regulatory approval.

### [Hostile interaction design: AI, governance, and the quest for human oversight](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-026-03065-y)
*AI & Society (Springer), May 11, 2026*

This paper examines how certain AI system designs may inadvertently or deliberately undermine meaningful human oversight, creating "hostile" interfaces that obscure decision-making processes or manipulate human judgment. The authors analyze governance frameworks and propose design principles that preserve genuine human agency in AI-assisted decision-making. This research is crucial as it addresses a fundamental challenge in AI alignment: ensuring that human oversight remains effective rather than becoming a mere illusion of control.
