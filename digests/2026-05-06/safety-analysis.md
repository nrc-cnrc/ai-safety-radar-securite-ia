# Blogs & News (2026-05-06)

## Top Stories

**[Model Spec Midtraining: Improving How Alignment Training Generalizes](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/R3Rrw8EscuRKxMFTz/model-spec-midtraining-improving-how-alignment-training)** introduces a new training technique where models learn about their intended behavior specifications between pre-training and alignment fine-tuning. By training on synthetic documents explaining the Model Spec, researchers can control how models generalize from subsequent alignment training—demonstrating that two identically fine-tuned models can develop different values depending on their midtraining specifications. This represents a significant methodological advance for controlling AI alignment outcomes and reducing agentic misalignment.

**[The AI Ad-Hoc Prior Restraint Era Begins](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/QX2ZCfkpWGqkyvStN/the-ai-ad-hoc-prior-restraint-era-begins)** reports that the White House has ordered Anthropic to halt expansion of access to "Mythos" and is considering a complete shift to requiring government permission before releasing highly capable AI models. This would mark a dramatic reversal from previous U.S. policy that avoided laying groundwork for systematic pre-release controls. The move signals a potential fundamental change in how frontier AI development will be regulated, shifting from post-hoc oversight to pre-approval requirements.

**[Import AI 455: AI systems are about to start building themselves](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-455-automating-ai-research)** examines emerging developments in automated AI research, describing it as "the first step towards recursive self improvement." While specific details aren't provided in the excerpt, this signals growing attention to AI systems that can improve their own capabilities or develop new AI systems. This development is crucial because recursive self-improvement represents a potential pathway to rapid capability gains that could outpace safety measures.

**[The distillation panic](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/the-distillation-panic)** discusses what the author calls "distillation attacks," critiquing the terminology around current concerns about model distillation techniques. The piece appears to address growing worries about how knowledge distillation from larger to smaller models might circumvent safety measures or enable unauthorized capability transfer. This matters because distillation techniques could potentially undermine efforts to control access to advanced AI capabilities through model size or access restrictions.

**[Interpreting Language Model Parameters](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/eAQZaiC3PcBhS4HjM/linkpost-interpreting-language-model-parameters)** introduces Adversarial Parameter Decomposition (VPD), a new method for understanding how language model parameters encode information and behaviors. The researchers claim VPD significantly improves on previous techniques and is now ready for application to larger, more relevant models. This advance in mechanistic interpretability could provide crucial insights into how AI systems store and process information, potentially enabling better detection of misaligned behaviors.

## Policy & Governance

**[CAISI Signs Agreements Regarding Frontier AI National Security Testing With Google DeepMind, Microsoft and xAI](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/caisi-signs-agreements-regarding-frontier-ai-national-security-testing)** announces new partnerships between the U.S. AI Safety Institute and major AI companies for national security-focused testing of frontier AI systems. This expands government oversight into potential national security risks from advanced AI capabilities and establishes formal channels for pre-deployment security assessments.

**[Partnering with Microsoft to strengthen frontier AI safety](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/partnering-with-microsoft-to-strengthen-frontier-ai-safety)** details a new collaboration between the UK AI Safety Institute and Microsoft to enhance safety measures for advanced AI systems. This partnership represents continued international coordination on AI safety governance and demonstrates how government safety institutes are working directly with major AI developers.

**[EU and Japan accelerate cooperation on AI, data, quantum and chips](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/eu-and-japan-accelerate-cooperation-ai-data-quantum-and-chips)** announces expanded international cooperation between the EU and Japan on AI governance and related technologies. This strengthens the international governance framework around AI development and suggests growing coordination among democratic allies on AI policy approaches.
