# Daily Digest (2026-05-06)

## Top 3 Developments

**CAISI Signs National Security Testing Agreements with Major AI Companies**: The [U.S. AI Safety Institute signed formal agreements](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/caisi-signs-agreements-regarding-frontier-ai-national-security-testing) with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI to conduct national security-focused testing of frontier AI systems before deployment. This matters because it establishes government oversight mechanisms for advanced AI capabilities that could pose national security risks, marking a significant expansion of pre-deployment safety evaluation requirements.

**Model Spec Midtraining Breakthrough Enables Fine-Grained Alignment Control**: [New research demonstrates](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/R3Rrw8EscuRKxMFTz/model-spec-midtraining-improving-how-alignment-training) that training models on their intended behavior specifications between pre-training and fine-tuning allows precise control over how alignment generalizes, with identically fine-tuned models developing different values based on their midtraining specifications. This matters because it provides a concrete method for reducing agentic misalignment and controlling AI system values, addressing a fundamental challenge in AI alignment.

**White House Orders Halt to Advanced AI Model Access Expansion** (ongoing): [Reports indicate](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/QX2ZCfkpWGqkyvStN/the-ai-ad-hoc-prior-restraint-era-begins) the White House has ordered Anthropic to halt expansion of access to "Mythos" and is considering requiring government permission before releasing highly capable AI models, marking a potential shift from post-hoc oversight to pre-approval requirements. This matters because it represents a dramatic policy reversal that could fundamentally reshape how frontier AI development is regulated and controlled.

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**

**Government pre-approval requirements for AI model releases could begin under reported White House policy changes**, as indicated by [orders halting Anthropic's model access expansion](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/QX2ZCfkpWGqkyvStN/the-ai-ad-hoc-prior-restraint-era-begins) and consideration of systematic pre-release controls. **Compositional vulnerability attacks demonstrate how coding agents can be manipulated through innocuous sequential requests**, as shown in [MOSAIC-Bench research](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03952v1) revealing 199 three-stage attack chains that bypass individual safety reviews. **Self-optimizing agent systems introduce alignment drift risks through automated improvement loops**, as evidenced by [OpenAI cookbook implementations](https://github.com/openai/openai-cookbook/pull/2654) of live trace-driven agent enhancement without explicit alignment preservation mechanisms.

**Decreased Risks**

**Enhanced alignment control through model spec midtraining enables precise value specification**, as demonstrated in [research showing](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/R3Rrw8EscuRKxMFTz/model-spec-midtraining-improving-how-alignment-training) how identical fine-tuning can produce different values based on midtraining specifications. **Government oversight expansion through formal national security testing agreements**, as established by [CAISI partnerships](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2026/05/caisi-signs-agreements-regarding-frontier-ai-national-security-testing) with major AI companies for pre-deployment safety evaluation. **Improved clinical AI trust calibration through atomic fact-checking methods**, as shown by [randomized trial results](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03916v1) increasing appropriate clinician trust from 26.9% to 66.5%.
