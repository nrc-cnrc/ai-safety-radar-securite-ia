# Blogs & News (2026-06-17)

## Top Stories

**[Predicting LLM Safety Before Release by Simulating Deployment](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/xPXJfgqFTvuJxGZbE/predicting-llm-safety-before-release-by-simulating)** - A new research paper describes methods for simulating model deployments before release as part of pre-deployment safety reviews, complementing existing targeted evaluations and red-teaming approaches. This represents a significant methodological advance for anticipating real-world risks before models reach users.

**[Agentic coding and persistent returns to expertise](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-code-expertise)** - Anthropic analyzes ~400,000 Claude Code sessions from October 2025-April 2026, finding that humans typically handle planning decisions while Claude executes implementation, revealing patterns in human-AI collaboration for coding tasks. This large-scale empirical data provides crucial insights into how AI coding assistants are actually being used and their productivity impacts.

**[Synthetic document finetuning for instilling positive traits](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/GTYJRLhqztxKF2v5R/synthetic-document-finetuning-for-instilling-positive-traits)** - Google DeepMind researchers adapt existing methods to train Gemini 3 Flash with specific traits by first midtraining on documents describing those properties, then finetuning on synthetic chat data demonstrating them. This work advances practical techniques for value alignment and trait specification in large language models.

**[Guardian Angels: LLM Personalization for Productivity and Security](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/siWqHqCSybdhtWGud/guardian-angels-llm-personalization-for-productivity-and)** - A proposal for "Guardian Angels" - personalized digital twin LLMs designed to enhance productivity while addressing cybersecurity and cognitive security challenges as powerful LLMs scale globally. This vision addresses critical gaps in how individuals might safely harness increasingly powerful AI systems.

**[Import AI 461: "Alignment is not on track"](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-461-alignment-is-not-on)** - Jack Clark's latest newsletter covers alignment progress concerns, FrontierCode developments, and synthetic research interns. The framing of alignment being "not on track" signals growing expert concern about the pace of safety progress relative to capability advancement.

## Policy & Governance

**[AI Now Co-Executive Director Sarah Myers West Testifies Before Senate Banking Committee](https://ainowinstitute.org/general/ai-now-co-executive-director-sarah-myers-west-testifies-before-senate-banking-committee)** - Dr. Sarah Myers West testified on June 11 at a Senate Banking Committee hearing on "AI and the American Dream," highlighting economic risks posed by the AI industry to the US economy and broader public. This testimony represents growing legislative attention to AI's economic disruption potential beyond just technical safety concerns.

**[Leviathan Waking](https://www.hyperdimensional.co/p/leviathan-waking)** - Dean Ball analyzes the relationship between Anthropic and the US government, suggesting a new era in AI governance is emerging. This commentary points to significant shifts in how AI labs are interfacing with government entities, potentially indicating more direct regulatory oversight.

**[2026 State of the Digital Decade report](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/2026-state-digital-decade-report-shows-progress-urges-closing-structural-gaps-reach-2030-goals)** - The European Commission's latest Digital Decade report shows progress but identifies structural gaps that must be closed to reach 2030 digital transformation goals. This EU assessment provides important context for how AI development fits within broader European digital strategy objectives.
