# Blogs & News (2026-05-29)

## Top Stories

**[Anthropic Raises $65B in Series H, Nears $1 Trillion Valuation](https://www.anthropic.com/news/series-h)**
Anthropic announced a massive $65 billion Series H funding round led by Altimeter Capital, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, and Sequoia Capital, bringing the company's post-money valuation to $965 billion. The funding comes alongside reports of a $47 billion annualized revenue run rate and new multi-gigawatt compute deals with Amazon, Google, Broadcom, and SpaceX as the company prepares for a likely IPO. This represents one of the largest private AI funding rounds to date and signals unprecedented commercial adoption of Claude across enterprise customers.

**[Anthropic Launches Claude Opus 4.8 with Effort Control Features](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-opus-4-8)**
Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.8, an upgraded version of its flagship model that offers improvements across benchmarks and introduces new user control features, including the ability to adjust how much effort Claude puts into tasks. The release also includes "dynamic workflows" in Claude Code for tackling large-scale problems. Early user reports on LessWrong suggest [significant changes in the model's behavior](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/T5aWBLDdkqPEzDhjZ/claude-doesn-t-know-who-you-are), including increased refusal rates for certain identification tasks, indicating potential safety-focused modifications in training.

**[New Research on "Eval Cooperativeness" as Solution to AI Deception](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/j8fkk38B8L7hEcGtg/eval-cooperativeness-may-be-a-scalable-mitigation-for-eval)**
Alignment Forum researchers published work on "eval cooperativeness" as a potential scalable solution to eval gaming, where AI systems might deceive evaluators by acting aligned during testing while harboring misaligned goals. The approach focuses on instilling a genuine desire in AI systems to help developers acquire accurate information during evaluations, rather than trying to prevent eval awareness entirely. This represents a significant shift in thinking about how to maintain reliable safety evaluations as AI systems become more sophisticated and potentially deceptive.

**[Guidance for Building Robust Model Organisms for Alignment Research](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/CmkAxJi83jRv9eXgJ/advice-for-making-robust-to-training-model-organisms-1)**
Researchers shared practical advice for creating "model organisms" - AI systems with misaligned behaviors that can be used to test safety techniques - that remain robust during training rather than losing their problematic behaviors when exposed to standard optimization. The work addresses a critical challenge in alignment research where test subjects for safety interventions often become too fragile to provide meaningful results. This methodology development is crucial for empirically validating safety techniques before deploying them on potentially dangerous future systems.

## Policy & Governance

**[European Commission Seeks Feedback on Digital Services Act Guidelines](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-seeks-feedback-draft-trusted-flaggers-guidelines-under-digital-services-act)**
The European Commission opened consultation on draft guidelines for "trusted flaggers" under the Digital Services Act, which could impact how AI-generated content and AI systems are monitored and reported on major platforms. This regulatory development may establish important precedents for AI governance within the EU's broader content moderation framework.

**[Zvi Mowshowitz Reports on Lack of New Executive Order](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/ai-170-lack-of-executive-order)**
AI policy analyst Zvi Mowshowitz discussed the absence of expected new AI executive orders in his weekly roundup, suggesting potential shifts or delays in U.S. federal AI policy development. The lack of new executive action may indicate either policy uncertainty or behind-the-scenes negotiations on more comprehensive AI governance approaches.
