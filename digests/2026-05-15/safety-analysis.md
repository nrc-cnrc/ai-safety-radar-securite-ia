# Blogs & News (2026-05-15)

## Top Stories

**Palisade Research demonstrates AI-powered autonomous hacking capabilities** - [Palisade Research](https://palisaderesearch.org/blog/intercode-ctf) published new findings showing that LLM agents can solve 95% of cybersecurity capture-the-flag challenges using simple prompting strategies, with 100% success rates in several attack categories including web exploitation and binary exploitation. Separately, [Google reported](https://www.rappler.com/technology/zero-day-exploit-ai-powered-cybersecurity-threats) the first known zero-day exploit developed with AI assistance, where attackers used large language models to find and weaponize previously unknown vulnerabilities. This represents a significant escalation in AI-enabled offensive cybersecurity capabilities that could lower barriers to sophisticated cyberattacks.

**UK AISI releases paper warning about automated alignment challenges** - The UK AI Safety Institute published research, [summarized on LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/gpuYFbMNH8PJXpmny/automated-alignment-is-harder-than-you-think-1), arguing that using AI research agents to solve AI alignment faces fundamental difficulties, particularly around evaluation realism and the safe-to-dangerous capability shift. The paper suggests that even non-scheming AI agents may struggle to provide reliable safety assurances for more advanced systems due to distributional gaps between evaluation and deployment. This challenges optimistic timelines for automating alignment research and highlights critical gaps in our evaluation methodologies.

**Anthropic releases strategic analysis on US-China AI competition** - [Anthropic published](https://www.anthropic.com/research/2028-ai-leadership) "2028: Two scenarios for global AI leadership," outlining their perspective on the AI competition between the US and China, emphasizing the importance of democratic nations maintaining leadership in frontier AI development. The analysis comes as the company also announced a [$200 million partnership with the Gates Foundation](https://www.anthropic.com/news/gates-foundation-partnership) for global health and development applications. This reflects growing recognition among AI labs that geopolitical considerations are becoming central to AI development and deployment strategies.

**New research highlights persistent vulnerabilities in AI safety fine-tuning** - Multiple studies from Palisade Research demonstrate continued fragility in AI safety measures, including [BadGPT-4o](https://palisaderesearch.org/blog/badgpt-4o) which strips safety guardrails from GPT-4o through simple fine-tuning attacks, and [Badllama 3](https://palisaderesearch.org/blog/badllama-3) showing safety fine-tuning can be removed in minutes. An [Alignment Forum post](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/tK8vqHDxaRGcysNJQ/the-safe-to-dangerous-shift-is-a-fundamental-problem-for-1) further explores how the safe-to-dangerous capability shift undermines evaluation realism, as models may behave safely during testing but dangerously during deployment. These findings suggest that current safety fine-tuning approaches provide insufficient protection against determined adversaries with model access.

## Policy & Governance

**IAPS releases national security policy recommendations following recent AI developments** - The Institute for AI Policy & Strategy published ["After Mythos: A National Security Playbook for Frontier AI,"](https://www.iaps.ai/research/after-mythos-a-national-security-playbook-for-frontier-ai) proposing strategic policy responses to address national security risks from advanced AI systems as the White House considers new executive actions. The memo appears to respond to recent frontier AI developments and emphasizes matching policy responses to the scale and urgency of emerging AI capabilities. This represents continued momentum in translating AI safety research into concrete policy recommendations for government decision-makers.
