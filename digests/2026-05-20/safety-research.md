# Research Papers (2026-05-20)

## Key Papers

### Operational Responsibility in AI Governance: A User-Centric Liability Framework
This peer-reviewed paper in AI and Ethics argues that AI should be understood as an instrument whose outputs remain the responsibility of human operators rather than developers, proposing a "user-centric governance" framework grounded in tort law and agency law. This matters because it offers a novel approach to the critical question of AI liability and could significantly reshape how we assign responsibility when AI systems cause harm.

### [Awakening the Hydra: Stabilizing Multi-Concept Backdoor Injection in Text-to-Image Diffusion Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19698v1)
This work demonstrates how multiple backdoor triggers can coexist and accumulate in text-to-image diffusion models through sequential fine-tuning, with the authors developing methods to stabilize these multi-concept backdoors by resolving semantic conflicts. This is concerning for AI safety as it shows how backdoors can persist and even strengthen through the common practice of model reuse and fine-tuning.

### [Be Kind, Rewrite: Benign Projections via Rewriting Defend Against LLM Data Poisoning Attacks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19147v1)
Researchers propose using LLM rewriting with "open-book benign samples" as a proactive defense against backdoor attacks in training data, showing theoretical guarantees that this approach can reduce poisoning probability. This matters because it provides a promising new defense mechanism against data poisoning attacks that threaten the integrity of LLM training.

### [DECOR: Auditing LLM Deception via Information Manipulation Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19270v1)
This paper introduces a multi-agent framework for detecting strategic deception in LLMs, grounding their approach in Information Manipulation Theory to identify subtle forms of deception like selective omission and focus shifting. This is significant for AI alignment as it provides tools to detect sophisticated deceptive behaviors that go beyond simple refusal or obvious lies.

### [Measuring Safety Alignment Effects in Autonomous Security Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19722v1)
The authors present a trace-based benchmark comparing safety-aligned language models with their uncensored versions when operating as autonomous security agents, finding behavioral differences that single-turn refusal benchmarks cannot capture. This matters for AI governance because it reveals how safety alignment may behave differently in agentic contexts compared to conversational ones.

### [Hallucination as Exploit: Evidence-Carrying Multimodal Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19192v1)
This work reframes multimodal agent hallucinations as authorization failures rather than mere answer-quality errors, proposing "evidence-carrying multimodal agents" that treat free-form model outputs as security-relevant claims requiring explicit evidence. This is crucial for AI safety as it addresses a fundamental vulnerability where false perceptual claims can trigger privileged actions in autonomous systems.

### [Token by Token, Compromised: Backdoor Vulnerabilities in Unified Autoregressive Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19227v1)
Researchers demonstrate the first multimodal backdoor attacks on unified autoregressive models, showing how triggers can propagate malicious effects across multiple output modalities due to shared parameters and vocabulary. This matters for AI security because these unified architectures are becoming more prevalent, and their shared components create new attack vectors that don't exist in unimodal models.

### [Detecting Fluent Optimization-Based Adversarial Prompts via Sequential Entropy Changes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19966v1)
The paper proposes a novel approach to detect adversarial prompts by monitoring token-level entropy changes as an online change-point detection problem, showing effectiveness against fluent optimization-based attacks that fool traditional perplexity-based detectors. This is important for AI safety as it provides a new defense against sophisticated jailbreaking attempts that maintain natural language fluency.
