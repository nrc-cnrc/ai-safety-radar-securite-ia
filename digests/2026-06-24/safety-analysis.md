# Blogs & News (2026-06-24)

## Top Stories

**[LLM-Driven Feature Discovery](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/WAZWA6FPQvH8okouJ/llm-driven-feature-discovery)** - Alignment Forum researchers present a new method for understanding AI model behaviors by using LLMs to automatically discover patterns and correlations in model transcripts across important distributions like deployment and evaluation scenarios. The approach splits transcripts into chunks, uses LLMs to extract behavioral features, and identifies novel behaviors or surprising correlations. This matters because automated feature discovery could significantly scale our ability to interpret and monitor advanced AI systems' behaviors.

**[Introducing Claude Tag](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-claude-tag)** - Anthropic launched Claude Tag, allowing Claude to join Slack channels as a team member with access to selected channels, tools, data, and codebases, while building context from channel conversations and planning multi-step tasks. This represents a significant step toward AI agents operating within existing workplace workflows and highlights the rapid deployment of agentic AI capabilities in enterprise settings.

**[GLM-5.2 Is The New Best Open Model](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/glm-52-is-the-new-best-open-model)** - Multiple expert commentators ([Zvi](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/glm-52-is-the-new-best-open-model), [Nathan Lambert](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/glm-52-is-the-step-change-for-open)) highlight GLM-5.2 as representing a capability threshold for open-source AI agents, with Lambert describing it as "the step change for open agents." This matters because advances in open-source agentic capabilities could accelerate AI deployment while making governance and safety oversight more challenging.

**[Superintelligence vs. The Second Strike](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/2kseP9fZghYHKLFno/superintelligence-vs-the-second-strike)** - A LessWrong analysis argues that nuclear deterrence will likely fail against superintelligent AI systems, as even nuclear-armed states will eventually be disempowered if they lack comparable AI capabilities to maintain credible second-strike guarantees. This matters because it suggests traditional security frameworks may be inadequate for managing superintelligence risks, requiring new approaches to international stability.

## Policy & Governance

**[Five Eyes Warn on AI Cyber Capabilities](https://www.channelnewsasia.com/business/five-eyes-intelligence-alliance-warns-new-ai-models-pose-urgent-cyber-risk-6202181)** - The Five Eyes intelligence alliance issued an urgent warning that emerging frontier AI models could rapidly multiply offensive hacking capabilities within months, specifically naming Anthropic's Mythos and OpenAI's GPT-5.5-Cyber as concerns. Officials urged faster vulnerability patching, limited network exposure, and defensive AI deployment in response. This matters because it represents the first major coordinated intelligence warning about specific AI models' cyber capabilities, potentially signaling more restrictive policies ahead.

**European AI Infrastructure Investment** - The European Commission approved [€76 million in German state aid](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-approves-eu76-million-german-state-aid-first-kind-semiconductor-testing-equipment) for semiconductor testing facilities and [€40 million for cable repair capacity](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-funds-first-regional-cable-hubs-and-launches-eu40-million-call-cable-repair-capacity), while launching an [AI Startup Award program](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/apply-ai-startup-award). This matters because it shows Europe's continued focus on building domestic AI infrastructure capabilities amid growing geopolitical tensions over technology access.
