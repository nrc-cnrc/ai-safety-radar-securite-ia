# Blogs & News (2026-07-02)

## Top Stories

**[Model access for third-parties — it's a big deal!](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/RuGZ5tMdqpnraJahJ/model-access-for-third-parties-it-s-a-big-deal)** - A LessWrong analysis argues that the growing gap between insider and outsider access to frontier AI models should be a top priority for the external AI safety community over the next 6-12 months. The post highlights concerns that employees at frontier labs have significantly better model access than external safety researchers and third-party auditors. This matters because unequal access could severely hamper independent safety research and oversight capabilities just when they're most needed.

**[A CERN for AI is a distraction; push for an IAEA instead](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/fPLCiCKjNiWhYD2mb/a-cern-for-ai-is-a-distraction-push-for-an-iaea-instead)** - This post argues against the popular "CERN for AI" proposal, suggesting that politically realistic versions would likely be ineffective catch-up labs that don't improve safety, while safety-enhancing versions (like pausing and merging companies) remain unrealistic. Instead, the author advocates for pursuing an international treaty with red lines now, followed by an IAEA-style verification body. This matters because it reframes the governance debate away from potentially counterproductive international collaboration models toward more focused regulatory approaches.

**[MIRI Newsletter #126](https://intelligence.org/2026/06/30/miri-newsletter-126/)** - MIRI announces the launch of "AI StopWatch," a new dedicated channel for daily AI news and analysis from MIRI's writers and analysts. The newsletter also summarizes MIRI's Technical Governance Team papers being presented at ICML 2026's Technical AI Governance Research workshop. This matters because it represents MIRI's expansion into more frequent public commentary and analysis, potentially increasing their influence on AI safety discourse.

**[Claude Sonnet 5 Is Not Frontier But Has Its Uses](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/claude-sonnet-5-is-not-frontier-but)** - Zvi Mowshowitz provides analysis of Claude Sonnet 5's capabilities, noting it's not a frontier model but has practical applications, with premium subscribers getting one week of access before token-based pricing kicks in. This matters as it provides independent assessment of new model capabilities outside of company marketing, helping calibrate expectations about AI progress trajectories.
