# Blogs & News (2026-07-13)

## Top Stories

**[The current bottleneck is political will, not research](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/EexsebbYhbe2gXkPP/the-current-bottleneck-is-political-will-not-research)** argues that AI safety has shifted from being research-constrained to being limited by lack of implementation of existing policy solutions. The author contends that best practices already exist but aren't being applied or enforced, and that a serious regulatory regime would be more impactful than additional research at this point. This represents a significant shift in thinking about AI safety priorities, suggesting the field should pivot toward advocacy and implementation rather than continued technical research.

**[5 "Plan A" scenarios](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/7HHKaD3BdsfHX7doz/5-plan-a-scenarios)** explores potential cooperative pathways between the US and China to slow transformative AI development, referencing the comprehensive framework at ai-2040.com. The post emphasizes the need for "layered defenses" covering governance (chips, weights, capabilities), verification (hardware, facilities, inspectors), and incentive structures. This matters because US-China cooperation represents one of the few plausible paths to global coordination on AI development timelines.

**[6 months to live for open models](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/6-months-to-live-for-open-models)** examines what Nathan Lambert describes as "the most serious test to date of open source AI's viability." While details are limited in the excerpt, this suggests mounting pressure on open-source AI development, potentially from regulatory or competitive forces. The timeline implications are significant for the broader AI governance landscape and the balance between open and closed AI development.

**[Independent alignment of language models](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/vPaXtarnJ37kGfPdJ/independent-alignment-of-language-models)** discusses how individual researchers can contribute to AI alignment through submitting feedback to companies like Anthropic, particularly regarding constitutional AI approaches that are designed to be "revised and improved over time." This highlights the potential for distributed, community-driven contributions to alignment research beyond formal academic or industry channels.

**[From wantons to moral agents](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/Z8kLbceGBMWB5HGfn/from-wantons-to-moral-agents)** explores the theoretical foundations for creating artificial agents that converge on shared moral principles through sufficient knowledge acquisition and reasoning. This theoretical work matters because it addresses fundamental questions about whether aligned AI systems can be grounded in objective moral reasoning rather than human preferences alone.
