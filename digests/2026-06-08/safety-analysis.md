## Top Stories

**[Statement: Anthropic warns of AI self-improvement risks, considers a pause](https://futureoflife.org/statement/statement-anthropic-warns-of-ai-self-improvement-risks/)** - Future of Life Institute

The Future of Life Institute released a statement highlighting Anthropic's warnings about AI self-improvement risks, with the company reportedly considering a pause in development. The statement emphasizes concerns about "approaching a runaway to superintelligence that could threaten our shared human future." This marks a significant shift for Anthropic, which has generally taken a more measured public stance on AI risks compared to other safety-focused organizations.

**[Can activation verbalizers surface an internal chain of thought?](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/QQQAcKuWK6k98FivY/can-activation-verbalizers-surface-an-internal-chain-of-1)** - LessWrong

Researchers evaluated whether activation verbalizers can reveal how AI models reason through math problems in a single forward pass, finding that while possible, the technique is not reliable for open-weight models. The work addresses a critical interpretability challenge: understanding AI reasoning when models don't externalize their thought processes through chain-of-thought prompting. This research is crucial for AI safety as it explores methods to monitor internal reasoning processes that may not be visible in model outputs.

**[How Far Apart Does a Model Think Its Tokens Are?](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Bxju8Fmpo2eW4oj9t/how-far-apart-does-a-model-think-its-tokens-are)** - LessWrong

A novel study examined how RoPE-based language models learn to perceive distances between tokens, revealing that models develop layer-specific position increments that show punctuation-based boundaries in early layers and concept-based grouping in deeper layers. While this modification doesn't affect performance, it provides insights into how models internally represent sequence structure. This work contributes to mechanistic interpretability efforts that are essential for understanding how advanced AI systems process information.

## Policy & Governance

**SpaceX-Google GPU Deal Creates New AI Infrastructure Dynamics** - The $11 billion annual contract between SpaceX and Google for Nvidia GPU capacity represents a significant shift in AI infrastructure, with SpaceX becoming a major compute provider to AI companies including Anthropic. This development could reshape the competitive landscape for AI development and raises questions about infrastructure concentration and access for smaller AI safety research organizations.

**Trump Administration Proposes AI Equity Stakes** - Reports indicate the Trump administration is considering taking equity positions in leading AI companies like OpenAI and Anthropic to create a national wealth fund that would distribute AI-generated profits to citizens. This proposal, reportedly supported by major AI CEOs, faces political opposition and raises concerns about government involvement in AI development, potential conflicts of interest, and the concentration of AI capabilities. The policy could significantly impact AI governance structures and the relationship between government and private AI development.
